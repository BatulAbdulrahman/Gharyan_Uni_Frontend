Department of Translation and interpretation

Find out the rest Collage specialties

The importance of translation and interpretation is increasing in the twenty-first century, the more the need to keep pace with the global development in various fields while preserving the linguistic and cultural identity, and the truth is that translation is not a product of the times, as museums testify to the oldest translation practices dating back to the fifth century BC. The symbols of the Rosetta Stone were translated and written in ancient Egyptian, Greek and hieroglyphs. There were also lots of patches and papyri translated from hieroglyphics to ancient Greek dating back to the fifteenth century BC, in addition to translations of the letters of Tel Amarna written in Akkadian and ancient Egyptian languages ​​that are found today in the major international museums.




Throughout the ages, the need for translation and transfer from one language to another is linked to civilizational and intellectual development.
All other peoples have known translation in order to expand their knowledge and add more knowledge and science through interaction with other cultures and peoples and the translation of their science and culture to benefit from them.
Translation has flourished remarkably since the beginning of the twentieth century, professional translators have emerged and the movement of educational and technological missions between countries has become active, and countries have been competing in the translation of science and knowledge in various fields to expand their sciences to keep pace with scientific progress and the latest discoveries and inventions.


The importance of translation


  • Exceeding geographical boundaries, transferring cultures, and turning the world into a small village
  • Keeping pace with civilizational and technological development
  • Translating thought streams and research methods helped navigate the various sciences
  • Translation helped to discuss global issues extensively and to identify different opinions
  • Develop people's perceptions about development issues
  • It allows countries to establish interactive relationships in various fields
  • Expansion of import and export operations of products and goods between countries and countries
  • Transfer global news and events
  • Promote and encourage global tourism

The study of translation in Universities takes 4 years, and the content of the collage program for the major differs from one university to another, where the student can get acquainted with the details of the academic subjects in each academic year by visiting the official website of the university in which he wishes to register to study.

The contents of the collage program for the translation and interpretation major

The first study year

  • Introduction to translation studies
  • Read and write critical
  • Society and culture
  • Textual analysis and effective communication
  • Politics and economics
  • Textual analysis and academic writing

The second study year

  • Technical translation
  • English literature
  • Linguistics
  • World civilizations
  • Translation techniques
  • Media and advertising translation

The third study year

  • Translation theories
  • Interpretation
  • Literary translation

Fourth study year

  • Translation history
  • Translation critique
  • Graduation project in one of the topics oftranslation and interpretation
